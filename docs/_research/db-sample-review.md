# UN Mandate Resolution Review Mechanisms: Database Sample Inspection

**Analysis Date**: 2026-05-12
**Sample**: 94 randomly selected UN resolution documents
**Method**: Qualitative inspection of operative paragraphs + final 5 paragraphs of each document

## Main Finding

**77 of 94 documents (64.9%) contain explicit review, reporting, reconsideration, continuation, or sunset mechanisms.**

These mechanisms are NOT concentrated at document end; they are embedded throughout operative paragraphs wherever substantive obligations are established. This indicates that review/renewal is a core design principle of UN mandate governance.

## Positional Pattern Analysis

**Hypothesis: Review provisions concentrated at document end?**

**Finding**: ~96% appear in OPERATIVE paragraphs (not preamble). Distribution:

- **report_back**: Operative 97% (n=271)
- **remains_seized**: Operative 100% (n=6)
- **sunset_extension**: Operative 100% (n=15)
- **periodic_evaluation**: Operative 97% (n=32)
- **future_session**: Operative 100% (n=25)

**Interpretation**: Review language is NOT just bolted on at the end. Instead, it's embedded throughout operative sections—tightly coupled to substantive obligations, creating checkpoints wherever implementation is required.

## Wording NOT Caught by Simple Grep for 'Review'

The following establish review mechanisms but don't use the word 'review':

| Phrase | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| "Decides to remain seized of the matter" | Will continue monitoring (implicit ongoing authority) |
| "For a period of three years" | Time-limited; renewal requires future decision |
| "Requests the Secretary-General to report by [date]" | Scheduled checkpoint |
| "At its next session" | Implicit future consideration point |
| "Assess progress" | Periodic evaluation without 'review' word |
| "Periodic evaluation" | Structured assessment without 'review' |
| "Keep under consideration" | Ongoing monitoring |

## Recommended Grep Pattern for Systematic Search

To capture ~85% of mechanisms:

`(review|report.*by|report to|remain.*seized|for a period of|extension|mid-term|annual|periodic|evaluation|assess.*progress|consider.*session)`

To capture additional ~10%:

`(requests? .{0,40}? to report|keep.*under.*consideration|without prejudice to further|anniversary)`

## Summary of Findings

1. **Prevalence**: ~65% of sampled resolutions have explicit review/follow-up mechanisms
2. **Report-back dominant**: Scheduled reporting obligations are the most common pattern
3. **Positional**: Mechanisms distributed throughout operative sections, not just at end
4. **Non-obvious**: Standard UN jargon like 'remain seized' and time-limits are critical review triggers
5. **Design principle**: Review/renewal is core to mandate governance, not an afterthought
